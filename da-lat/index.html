27 Oct 2014 • on Vietnam • by Kaitlyn Richards

Da Lat

While trying to decided which cities we should visit in Vietnam, we came across Da Lat. It's a mountain town, with cooler temperatures than what we've been experiencing so far; it's known as the "City of Eternal spring".

We took a bus there from Ho Chi Minh City, which was supposed to take around six hours but ended up being more like eight and a half. The scenery outside was super pretty, though! So many hills and valleys.

Once we arrived, we wandered around to find somewhere to stay. We had found one online that we liked, but hadn't been able to get ahold of them before arriving, and unfortunately they were full. However, we found a nice place just up the street. The ladies working there didn't speak a ton of English, which proved challenging later when we tried to add another night - she ended up calling a friend on the phone and we used her as a translator, passing the phone back and forth!

After the sun went down at night, it was definitely a lot colder than what we're used to! We even had to break out the sweaters when going for dinner. It was weird seeing all the locals walking around in their winter jackets and toques. It seemed like every second store sold primarily winter jackets and accessories; there were a lot of North Face knockoff stores. It's definitely a vacation town for a lot of Vietnamese; we were there over a weekend and saw tons of families walking around with small children. On weekends they close off an area to vehicles and have a concert. We saw local teams of rollerbladers around as well - it seems to be the local activity for kids/teenagers.

Da Lat was also where I had the most delicious cinnamon French toast with fresh banana and mango. It was so good. I had it for breakfast twice, and I've been dreaming about it ever since.

We hit up the main market one day while walking around. Lots of special fruits and things that only grow near Dalat, so apparently the Vietnamese that visit tend to go back with bags full of foods they've bought. We got some candies from a friendly lady in the market, but as usual mostly just wandered around taking in the sights and smells.

For our last day in Da Lat, we wanted to cram in a lot of sights that we were wanting to see but hadn't seen yet. We couldn't find a tour that had all the things we wanted to do, and they were all expensive anyway, so we decided to just walk everywhere.

We started off by going to Crazy House, which is a weird architectural house. It's actually a guest house as well, so you can stay there if you want. Unfortunately, after we got there we learned that it is closed on the weekends. You could see a bit of it from outside, but not a whole lot.

After that we walked up to Bao Dai Palace. This was his summer palace. It's an interesting looking art deco style building that isn't particularly exciting from the outside, but is kind of odd in places. The inside was a pretty cool look at how he lived. Various members of the family were around until only 5-15 years ago. We had to put bags over our shoes to walk around so that we didn't dirty up the place.

This was a lot of walking to start with, but it was only the beginning! We then walked through the countryside winding around the lanes up to the cable car, and took that down towards the lake.

It was kind of weird to be taking a cable car down; usually it's going up! I was a bit scared about the height...luckily I was facing up the mountain and Alan was facing downwards.

We were originally planning on looking around the lake and the pagoda there, but it was getting a bit late and we also wanted to go to the Datanla waterfall. Since we weren't sure what time it closed, we decided to skip the lake and pagoda and walk straight over to the waterfall.

Now, the reason this waterfall is so popular is that it has a mini rollercoaster from the entrance down to the first falls! You're in two-person cars and you get to control the speed, but it was still pretty scary for someone who hates rollercoasters (that someone would be me).

We heard that the waterfall itself wasn't that impressive, but we thought it was pretty nice! There are three falls, with a cable car to the second and an elevator to the third, but we only went to the first as we didn't have a whole lot of time.

You also ride the rollercoaster back up to the top; it just pulls you up a really steep incline.

Walking the 6.5km back to town after having already walked 9km earlier in the afternoon didn't sound like that much fun (especially since it was getting dark), so we got a taxi back into town. Just in time, too - it started pouring rain as soon as we got back!

Kaitlyn Richards
