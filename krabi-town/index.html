24 Sep 2014 • on Thailand • by Alan Richards

Krabi Town

Krabi is actually a province of Thailand, at the centre of which lies Krabi Town. We arrived here initially from Bangkok, but went straight to Ao Nang. See our previous post for details of the beaches there.

Krabi Town is a much different place. Although still catering extensively to the many tourists that pass through, for many it is just a stop on the way to the beaches and resorts. For us, though, we spent 6 nights here in the end - split between two different visits. It's a much nicer, quieter place than Ao Nang, but of course missing the scenery and beach. Accommodation and food prices are cheaper, and you no longer have to be accosted to buy a suit or trinket every few steps along the street.

We stayed at the "meh" P.N Guesthouse ($10 for fan room with private bathroom - tiny though), as well as the nicer and roomier i Talay Guesthouse ($10 for fan room with shared bathroom). Fan rooms have been fine for us in Thailand, and in the quieter rainy season shared bathrooms seem to often be private anyway.

Things to do:

We rented a scooter 3 times while in Krabi Town. Rentals are quick and easy from many many places around town and usually run 150-200 baht for a day. It was my first time driving a scooter, but it didn't take long to get the hang of it. It meant we can visit sites on our own time, stop at interesting places along the way, and even look for a few geocaches :)

One of the first places we visited was Wat Tham Sua, also known as the Tiger Cave Temple. This is a shrine/temple at the top of a limestone peak. It was pretty hot out, so Kaitlyn decided to stay at the Wat at the bottom (she didn’t think she could make it up with her asthma), but I made the climb to the top. Sort of like a mini grouse grind in a sauna really. Took me just over an hour up and down, including quite a long time at the top cooling down and taking tons of pictures. You climb 1267 steps up to the top, and in some places it gets quite steep. The views from the top are great, and although it was a little hazy and cloudy the day we went, I got good views of Krabi Town out towards the Andaman Sea and the surrounding countryside.

We did a day trip out to Ao Nang for another soak in the sea, to get a massage, and also to grab some of the geocaches (most of which are located around the town).

I also noticed an interesting looking geocache just off the road between Krabi and Ao Nang. It involved driving up a side road through pineapple fields and various tree plantations. We then turned up a dirt track for a kilometre or so and hiked 100m through the forest to suddenly arrive at a huge white limestone arch. The karst formations here are cool, but many are hidden by the jungle or farms. Definitely something we would never have found or heard about otherwise.

The photo is hard to see, but look for the large white arch in the background behind the trees.

Within Krabi itself there are a lot of restaurant choices. We had a lot of Pad Thai, fried rice, and red and green curries. Also some good italian pasta. One of my favourite places was Mr. Krabi. Good western food and a nice ambiance - the pizza we had was great. Mr. Krabi is very friendly, and Kaitlyn loved the bottomless popcorn they give you!

The cheapest and easiest food was just down the street by the pier at the night market. We ate here a few times - you just sit at a table in the square and order from the stalls around. We also came quite a few nights to get banana pancakes/rotee for dessert. Delicious!
