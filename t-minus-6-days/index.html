20 Aug 2014 • by Alan Richards

T minus 6 days

Last night we left town late after our day of cleaning, and headed off up the sea to sky for a couple nights of camping on the back way to Kamloops. Even though it's a weeknight, the popular campgrounds (Porteau Cove and Alice Lake) were all full, and so we continued on past Squamish up to Whistler. We stopped at Cal-Cheak Recreation Site, and discovered a beautiful, well-kept, quiet little camping area only a few hundred meters from the highway and right by the river. Just what we wanted for a quick overnight stop.

This morning, we drove back down the road a minute or two to Brandywine Falls. We've never actully stopped here before on our many trips to Whistler, and this was well worth the stop. The 3rd highest waterfall in BC I believe, and a good example of some lava flows (we found the nearby educational earthcache).

We made a short stop in Whistler for groceries for a picnic, and then stopped just before Pemberton at Nairn Falls. This is another provincial park, and the 3rd major waterfall on the sea to sky. Very different to the other two, as this is less about the height of the waterfall and more the cool erosion of the rock and meandering waterway. We had a quick rain shower pass by right near the falls and we had to hide out under some trees for 10 minutes while it went by.

Continuing through Pemberton we started up the Duffey Lake road, passing by Joffre Lakes (we'll have to save that for another time), and Duffey Lake. Dropping into Lillooet we pulled off to check out the views of the BC Hydro resevoir here, and then stopped for groceries for dinner.

We camped tonight at Marble Canyon on my brother's recommendation. A nice enough Provincial Park along the lake, and the tent sites are set away from the parking down by the lake. We made a nice dinner and enjoyed paddling in the lake. As we cleaned up though we could see a storm with lightning and thunder coming down the valley. We hurried to put things away and get ready for bed, and made it into the tent just as the rain arrived. 5 minutes later the torrential downpour started, and we realised it was hailing. The tent held up well, although a lot of hail bounced under the fly and it got a bit cold. The hail soon melted and turned to rain, until we realised there was 2 inches of water sitting in our tent pad not draining away properly. The edges of the tent were floating, and a few flip flops floated away, but again our tent held up admirably and we didn't get wet. The rain eased off and the ground started to drain, so we drifted off to sleep thinking the storm was over...

But at around midnight, we were awakened by a park ranger yelling down the embankment. Apparently a mudslide came down the hill just around the corner, and they were concerned about a second slide possibly near the campsite. We packed up the soaking wet tent in the dark and bundled everything into the car and headed down the road, along with the rest of the campground. In nearby Cache Creek, we learnt the highway was now closed between Lillooet and Cache Creek, and would remain closed for most of the next day. Since we were so close to Kamloops, and didn't really feel like setting up the wet tent, we opted to just drive the rest of the way, and sleep in a real bed. An adventurous day in the end!
